Ziyue Jia is currently an MSc Clinical Pharmacology student at King’s College London, with a BSc in Pharmaceutics and a strong foundation in pharmacology. Ziyue has authored and co-authored several research publications and patents.

Ziyue's experience spans basic and clinical pharmacology. He has applied his skills in R&D settings, leading and collaborating on projects that bridge lab research with real-world applications. He has experience in in vivo and in vitro experiments, including rodent disease models, cell culture assays, etc., covering treatments for osteoarthritis, inflammatory diseases, and cancer.

Passionate about translational medicine and drug development, he is continually aiming to bridge the gap between scientific research and clinical practice. By leveraging his multidisciplinary background in pharmacology and pharmaceutics, he aspires to contribute to the development of safer, more effective therapies. He is eager to engage with fellow professionals and organisations in the pharmaceutical and biomedical fields – whether in collaborative research, clinical trial design, or innovation-driven R&D – to advance human health and achieve meaningful outcomes.
